Getting started

REDnet runs on Matrix, an encrypted messaging protocol. You access it through Element — either the website your organizer gave you or the Element X app from your phone's app store. Both connect to the same account and the same rooms.

The sidebar

The left side of the screen has three areas:

  • Rooms — group conversations, like channels. Click a room name to open it
  • People (DMs) — private one-on-one conversations. Click the People icon or section to see them
  • Spaces — folders that group rooms together. Your community is a Space. Click its name to browse all rooms you have access to
Direct messages

A DM is a private conversation between you and one other person. DMs are encrypted the same way rooms are.

  • Find a DM — look under People in the sidebar. If someone has messaged you, their name appears there
  • Start a new DM — click the + or compose button near the People section, then type the person's username
  • Bot DM — you have a DM with @rednet-gov (the governance bot). This is where you report problems
Searching messages
  • Search bar — press Ctrl+K (or Cmd+K on Mac) to open search. Type a word or phrase to find messages across rooms
  • In-room search — click the search icon at the top of a room to search within that room only
  • Limitation — search works on the web and desktop app. The mobile app cannot search encrypted message history
First time? Open #welcome in the sidebar and read the pinned message. It has your recovery passphrase instructions. Then explore #general and say hello.
Your rooms

The community is organized into a Matrix Space, which groups rooms together. You were automatically added to the starter rooms when you joined.

Starter rooms
  • welcome — introductions and community announcements for new members
  • announcements — organizer broadcasts (you can read but not post)
  • general — open discussion for the whole community
  • reference — pinned information and resources (does not auto-delete)

Organizers may invite you to additional rooms or sub-spaces for specific working groups. You can see all rooms you belong to in the left sidebar.

Navigating rooms
  • Switch rooms — click a room name in the left sidebar
  • Find rooms — click the Space name at the top to browse all rooms in the community
  • Unread indicators — bold room names have unread messages; a red badge means you were mentioned
  • Room info — click the room name at the top of the chat to see the topic, members, and pinned messages
All community rooms use end-to-end encryption. Messages can only be read by verified members of that room. The server operator, hosting provider, and anyone who seizes the hardware cannot read encrypted messages.
Messaging
Sending messages

Type in the box at the bottom of a room and press Enter. Your message is encrypted before it leaves your device.

Formatting
  • Bold — wrap text in **double asterisks**
  • Italic — wrap text in *single asterisks*
  • Code — wrap text in `backticks`
  • Quote — start a line with >
  • Mention someone — type @ then their display name
Editing and deleting
  • Edit — hover over your message (or long-press on mobile), click the pencil icon, change the text, press Enter
  • Delete — hover over your message, click the three-dot menu, select "Remove." The message is replaced with "Message deleted" for everyone
Deleting removes from view, not from memory. Deleted messages are redacted on the server and removed from the timeline, but any member who saw the message before you deleted it may have already read it. Think before you send.
Replies and threads
  • Reply — hover over a message and click the reply arrow. Your response appears linked to the original
  • Threads — if available, click the thread icon to start a side conversation that keeps the main room uncluttered
Files and images

Click the attachment icon (paperclip or +) to send files, images, or documents. Files are encrypted in transit and at rest. Media is subject to the community's retention policy and may be automatically deleted after a set period.

Encryption

Every community room uses end-to-end encryption (E2EE). Your messages are encrypted on your device before they are sent, and only devices belonging to room members can decrypt them.

The shield icon

You may see a small shield icon next to messages or in room headers. This indicates the encryption status:

  • Green shield — message was sent from a verified device. This is the normal state
  • Red warning — message was sent unencrypted or from an unverified device. Report this to a moderator if you see it on another member's message
  • Grey shield — verification has not been completed for that device yet
Verifying sessions

When you log in on a new device, Element asks you to verify it against an existing session. This proves the new device belongs to you.

  1. Open Element on both devices
  2. On the new device, you will see a "Verify this session" prompt
  3. On your existing device, approve the verification request
  4. Compare the emoji or numbers shown on both screens
  5. If they match, confirm on both devices
If you cannot verify, use your recovery passphrase. If you no longer have access to another session, enter the recovery passphrase you saved during setup. It unlocks your message history and cross-signing keys. Without either method, you lose access to past encrypted messages.
"Unable to decrypt" messages

If you see "Unable to decrypt this message," it means your current device does not have the key for that message. Common causes:

  • New device not verified — verify your session to restore key access
  • Message sent before you joined — encryption keys are per-session; you cannot decrypt messages from before your first session in a room
  • Key backup not restored — check Settings > Security > Secure Backup and make sure it says "connected"
Privacy practices

Encryption protects message content. The practices below protect everything else: who you are, where you are, and what your device reveals.

Identity
Do not share your real name, location, or photo. Your username, display name, and avatar are visible to everyone in the community. Keep them disconnected from your real identity. Do not reuse a handle from any other platform. Do not upload a photo of yourself as your avatar.
Lock screen notifications
Turn off message previews on your lock screen. Anyone holding your phone can read notification content without unlocking it.

iPhone: Settings > Notifications > Element X > Show Previews > Never
Android: Settings > Apps > Element X > Notifications > Sensitive notifications off
Network hygiene
  • Link previews — the server has URL previews disabled. If you paste a link, only the raw URL appears (no embedded images or page titles)
  • Files you receive — do not open files from people you do not trust. Files sent in encrypted rooms are safe in transit, but the file itself could still be harmful
  • External links — clicking a link leaves the encrypted environment. The destination site can see your IP address and browser fingerprint
Message retention

Most rooms have automatic message retention enabled. Messages older than the retention period are automatically deleted from the server. The #reference room is exempt so that pinned resources persist. Do not treat retention as a guarantee that messages disappear from every device; members who were online may have cached copies locally.

Devices and recovery
Managing sessions

Open Settings > Security to see all devices where you are logged in. Each session is listed with its name, last active time, and verification status.

  • Sign out old devices — if you see a session you no longer use (a lost phone, an old laptop), sign it out from this list. This revokes its decryption keys
  • Rename sessions — give each session a name you recognize (e.g., "work laptop," "phone") so you can tell them apart
Second device
Log in on a second device. If you lose your phone, a second active session (laptop, tablet) lets you keep access without needing your recovery passphrase. Log in on the second device and verify it from your existing session.
Recovery passphrase

During setup you were given a recovery passphrase. It is your last-resort way to restore message history and cross-signing keys if you lose all your devices.

  • Store it offline — write it on paper or put it in a password manager. Not in a notes app, not in a screenshot, not in a text message
  • Never share it — no organizer, moderator, or admin will ever ask for your recovery passphrase. Anyone who asks is not who they claim to be
  • Test it — periodically confirm you still have access to it. If you lost it, contact an organizer to discuss recovery options
If you lose all devices AND your recovery passphrase, your encrypted message history is gone. There is no reset, no admin override, no recovery backdoor. This is by design.
Reporting problems

If something goes wrong, you have tools to act. You do not need to wait for a moderator to notice.

Report a compromised account

If you notice a member behaving strangely (messages they deny sending, unfamiliar activity, unknown devices), report it through your private channel with the governance bot:

  1. Open your direct messages (People section in the sidebar)
  2. Find @rednet-gov
  3. Type: !report @username --detail "what you observed"

You can also write the reason directly after the username:
!report @username suspicious login from new device

Reports are confidential. Only organizers and admins can see who filed a report. The person you report will not know you reported them. Reporting does not kick or ban anyone; it flags the account for review.
If your own account is compromised
Act fast.

1. Change your password immediately (Settings > Account > Password).
2. Sign out all other sessions (Settings > Security > sign out each one you don't recognize).
3. Tell an organizer or moderator by DM so they can check for damage.
4. If you cannot log in, contact an organizer through whatever channel the person who invited you used (text, in person, phone call).
Panic — wipe this device (under duress)
If you are forced to unlock your device or hand over your account, open your avatar menu (top-left) and choose Panic — wipe this device, then confirm.

It does two things at once: it erases this device — messages, encryption keys and your session are removed and it returns to a blank login screen — and it alerts your organizers, who lock your account so it can't be used from any device.

This is reversible. When you are safe, an organizer restores your access and your rooms and history come back — you log in again with your password and recovery passphrase.

If the menu item isn't there, you can do the same by sending !duress in your DM with @rednet-gov.
Content concerns

If someone posts personal information (real names, locations, photos of faces), alert a moderator by DM or mention them in the room. Moderators can delete messages. If the content is urgent and no moderator is online, use !report in your DM with @rednet-gov to create a permanent record — or right-click (or long-press) the message and choose Report to organizers, which does the same in one step.

Request a new room or space

Need a room for a project, working group, or region? You can't create rooms directly — every room here is set up encrypted and connected to the community by an organizer. Ask for one through your bot DM:

  1. Open your DM with @rednet-gov
  2. For a room: !gov request room "Kitchen Crew" --why "coordinate food runs"
  3. For a whole sub-space (a folder that groups several rooms — e.g. a region or chapter): !gov request space "Northwest Chapter" --why "regional coordination"

An organizer reviews it and you'll hear back in the same DM. If it's approved, you're invited automatically — and you become the room's moderator: you approve join requests, invite people, and pin what matters. Your request is private; other members never see it.

Joining rooms

Browse the community space to see what's there. Some rooms you can join right away; others show Ask to join — tap it and the room's moderator approves you. Some rooms are invite-only and won't appear at all until someone invites you.

Community roles

The community uses four roles. Your role determines what actions you can take. Most people are members.

  • Member — send messages, view rooms, report problems. This is you
  • Moderator — everything a member can do, plus kick, ban, delete messages, and run audits
  • Organizer — everything a moderator can do, plus mint invites, confirm vouches, and assign moderators
  • Admin — full control including revoking members and managing power levels

If you are asked to take on a moderator role, an organizer or admin will assign it. You will see your elevated permissions reflected in the room settings under "Roles & Permissions."

Assigned a new role? Read the moderator guide for your tools and responsibilities. Organizers and admins should also read the operator guide.
Troubleshooting
"Unable to decrypt" on old messages

Verify your session (see Encryption above). If you already verified, check Settings > Security > Secure Backup. If it says "not connected," enter your recovery passphrase to reconnect.

Not receiving notifications
  • Check in-app settings — Settings > Notifications. Make sure notifications are enabled for the rooms you care about
  • Check device settings — your phone's notification settings for the Element app may be blocking alerts
  • Background restrictions — some Android phones aggressively kill background apps. Check your battery optimization settings
Logged out unexpectedly

If the app logs you out (after an update, a device restart, or a server maintenance window), log back in with your username and password. If prompted to verify, use another active session or your recovery passphrase.

Cannot find a room

All rooms are inside the community Space. Click the Space name in the sidebar to browse rooms. If you still cannot find it, you may not have been invited to that room. Ask an organizer.

Need more help

Ask in #general or send a DM to the person who invited you. Moderators and organizers monitor the community rooms.
