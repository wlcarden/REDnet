Moderator guide

You help keep this community safe. This guide covers what you can do, how to do it, and when to ask an operator for help.

New to the platform? Read the member guide first for messaging basics, encryption, privacy practices, and device management. Review the safety guide to understand what the platform protects and what it doesn't. This guide covers moderation-specific tools.
Your role

You are PL 50 (moderator) in the rooms you've been assigned to. Operators are PL 100 and handle community-wide actions. Regular members are PL 0.

You can
Kick members from your rooms
Ban members from your rooms
Delete (redact) messages
Invite people to your rooms
Set room topics
You cannot
Mint registration invites
Add or remove moderators
Change room settings
Access governance rooms
Use Draupnir bot commands
Your scope is your rooms. Moderation actions you take only affect the rooms where you have PL 50. You cannot kick or ban someone from a room you do not moderate. If someone is causing problems across multiple rooms, escalate to an operator.
Your rooms

Your operator assigned you to specific rooms. You can see which rooms you moderate by checking your power level — open any room's settings and look under "Roles & Permissions." If your name appears at the "Moderator" level, you can act there.

Community rooms you can see
  • welcome — first thing members see
  • announcements — organizer broadcasts
  • general — open discussion
  • reference — pinned info, does not auto-delete

You may also be in compartment rooms (sub-spaces for specific groups). If so, you moderate those rooms the same way.

You do not have access to #vouch-log, #governance, or #rednet-mod. These are operator-only rooms for invite tracking, governance, and bot commands. If you need information from them, ask an operator.
Moderation actions

All moderation happens inside Element. No command-line tools are needed.

Delete a message
  1. Hover over (or long-press on mobile) the message
  2. Click the three-dot menu
  3. Select "Remove" (Element Web) or "Delete" (Element X)
  4. Confirm when prompted — the message is replaced with "Message deleted"
Kick a member
  1. Click the member's avatar or name to open their profile
  2. Click "Kick" and provide a reason
  3. The member is removed from that room but can rejoin if invited again
Ban a member
  1. Click the member's avatar or name to open their profile
  2. Click "Ban" and provide a reason
  3. The member is removed and cannot rejoin that room unless unbanned
Bans are per-room, not community-wide. Banning someone from #general does not remove them from #announcements or any other room. If someone needs to be removed from everything, escalate to an operator — they can use Draupnir to ban across all rooms at once.
Mute a member
  1. Click the member's avatar or name
  2. Click "Mute" — they can still read messages but cannot send new ones
  3. Unmute the same way when appropriate
Common situations
Someone posts personal information
Delete it immediately. Real names, locations, phone numbers, photos of faces, addresses — anything that could identify a community member. Delete the message, then DM the person to explain why. If it was a photo or file, it may persist in the media store until the retention period expires. Notify an operator if the information was highly sensitive.
Someone is disruptive

Delete the offending messages. If it continues, mute them. If muting does not resolve it, kick them from the room. Ban only if kicking fails to stop the behavior. Escalate: delete → mute → kick → ban → operator.

Someone you don't recognize appears

Every member joined through an attributed invite. If you see an unfamiliar username, that is normal — you may not know everyone. Do not kick someone just because you have not met them. If their behavior is suspicious, notify an operator. They can check the vouch graph to see who invited them.

You suspect a compromised account
Mute them in your rooms and escalate immediately. Do not ban — the operator needs to investigate and may need to revoke everyone that account vouched for. Send a DM to an operator with the username and what you observed.
Reporting via gov bot

The governance bot listens in #gov-bot for !gov commands. As a moderator you can run audits and report compromised accounts without needing server access.

Commands you can use
# Check network status: !gov status # Run coercion canary audit (PL 50+): !gov audit # Report a compromised account (any member can do this): !gov report @user --detail "what you observed"
Reporting a compromised account

If you suspect a member's account has been compromised (unfamiliar activity, messages they deny, unknown devices), report it immediately. The !gov report command posts an alert to both #gov-bot and the vouch log. An organizer or admin will review and take action.

Members report through a private DM. Regular members do not have access to #gov-bot. They use !report in a private DM with @rednet-gov. Reports filed this way appear in #gov-bot as alerts. The reporter's identity is visible to operators but not to the person being reported.
You can also use the governance dashboard. Open /governance/ in your browser — the Provenance tab shows quick-action buttons that copy the right command to your clipboard. Paste into #gov-bot and send.
When to escalate

Some situations need an operator. Do not try to handle these yourself.

Always escalate
  • Suspected compromise — a member's account behaving abnormally (sudden activity changes, messages they deny sending, unfamiliar devices in their session list)
  • Cross-room problems — someone causing issues in multiple rooms needs a community-wide response
  • Threats or safety concerns — anything suggesting physical danger to a community member
  • Leaked sensitive content — real identities, locations, or operational details posted in a room
  • Someone asking for access — members wanting to join rooms they're not in, or wanting moderator privileges
How to escalate

For suspected compromise, use !gov report @user --detail "..." in #gov-bot first — this creates an immediate alert. (Members without #gov-bot access can use !report in their DM with @rednet-gov.) Then send a direct message to an operator with the username, the room, and a brief description. If you cannot reach an operator by DM, post in #general asking them to check their messages.

Your security

You have more access than a regular member. The same advice that applies to everyone applies more to you.

Turn off lock screen notification previews. Moderation messages — ban reasons, reports, DMs from operators — can show on your lock screen if previews are on. Anyone holding your phone can read them.

iPhone: Settings > Notifications > Element X > Show Previews > Never
Android: Settings > Apps > Element X > Notifications > Sensitive notifications off
Practices
  • Second device — log in on a second device (laptop or tablet). If you lose your phone, you can still access the community.
  • Recovery passphrase — store it offline. Paper or a password manager that does not sync to the cloud.
  • Handle and display name — do not use your real name. Do not reuse a handle from another platform. The same rules that apply to members apply to you.
  • Report if compromised — if you suspect your device or account was accessed by someone else, tell an operator immediately. They can adjust your permissions and investigate.
