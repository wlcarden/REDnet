Messaging & rooms

Everyday conversation. All of it end-to-end encrypted.

Encrypted messages

Send and read messages only room members can decrypt. The server stores ciphertext it cannot read.

Where: the compose box in any room · how encryption works

Rooms & spaces

Group conversations, grouped into your community Space (a folder of rooms). You're added to the starter rooms on joining.

Where: the left sidebar · navigating rooms

Direct messages

Private one-to-one conversations, encrypted the same way rooms are.

Where: the People section · starting a DM

Formatting, edit & delete

Bold, italic, code, quotes and mentions with markdown. Edit or redact your own messages after sending.

Where: markdown in the box; hover a message → pencil / ⋯ → Remove · messaging

Replies, threads & files

Reply inline, branch a side thread, or send encrypted files and images.

Where: hover a message → reply / thread icon; paperclip to attach · messaging

Search

Find messages across rooms (web and desktop; the mobile app can't search encrypted history).

Where: Ctrl/Cmd + K · searching

Request a room or space

You can't create rooms directly — ask an organizer, and if approved you're invited automatically and become its moderator.

Where: + next to the room list → "Request a room or space" · requesting a room

Privacy & anti-forensics

What protects everything encryption doesn't — who you are, and what a seized server reveals.

Disappearing messages Automatic

Chat auto-deletes from the server after a few days (30 at most). A seized server reveals only a short window — that's the point.

Where: the pill in each room header shows its window · retention

Reference page

Durable, public-safe resources (crisis, legal, digital-security, mutual-aid) that never auto-delete. Keep sensitive specifics in chat instead.

Where: /reference, linked in the app

Identity hygiene

Your handle and avatar are visible to everyone in the community — keep them unlinked from your real name, face, or other platforms.

Where: your profile / Settings · privacy practices

Lock-screen previews off

Stop message content from showing on your locked screen, where it bypasses encryption.

Where: your phone's notification settings for Element X · how to disable

No public discovery Built in

The community isn't searchable, listed, or indexed — it's invite-only, and closed to outside federation. There's nothing to enable; it's how the server is built.

Where: server-enforced · what's protected

Safety & coercion

Tools for when something goes wrong — or when you're forced.

Panic — wipe this device

Under duress, one confirmed action erases this device (messages, keys, session) and alerts organizers, who lock your account. Fully reversible when you're safe.

Where: avatar menu (top-left) → "Panic — wipe this device" (or !duress to @rednet-gov) · under duress

Report to organizers

Flag a compromised account or harmful content, confidentially. The reported person is never notified; only organizers see who filed it.

Where: right-click / long-press a message → "Report to organizers" (or !report in your @rednet-gov DM) · reporting

Trust & roles

How membership is vetted, and who can do what.

Vouched invites

There are no open invite links — every member joins through a named voucher, recorded in an append-only trust record. Vouching for someone ties your name to theirs.

Where: a single-use invite from an organizer · roles

See who vouched

A member's profile shows who vouched for them — the trust chain, visible where you'd look someone up.

Where: click a member → their profile ("Vouched by …")

Roles & labels

Member, Moderator, Organizer, Admin — shown as a labeled chip by each name so you can see who's who.

Where: the member list and profiles · roles · moderator guide

Governance menu Organizers

Mint invites, review pending room requests (approve/deny), create rooms, and add members — all in-app, no console needed.

Where: the governance button in the left space panel · operator guide

Manage member & room lifecycle Organizers

Set a member's role, confirm a vouch, or revoke access; archive or delete a room from its menu.

Where: a member's profile → "Manage member"; a room's menu → Archive / Delete · operator guide

Account & recovery

Keeping access to your account and your history.

Recovery passphrase

Your last-resort key to restore message history and cross-signing if you lose every device. No one can reset it for you — store it offline.

Where: shown once at signup; Settings → Security · devices & recovery

Sessions & second device

A second logged-in device keeps you in without the passphrase. Sign out a lost or old device to revoke its keys.

Where: Settings → Security · managing sessions

Session verification

Prove a new login is really you by matching emoji/numbers against an existing session — this restores your key access.

Where: the "Verify this session" prompt on a new device · verifying sessions

First-run checklist

A one-time walkthrough of the safety basics: save your passphrase, kill lock-screen previews, keep your identity out of chat, know the retention window.

Where: shown once, right after your first login

Voice & video calls Not yet

The one thing REDnet deliberately doesn't do — yet.

Group calls aren't available. REDnet is text and files for now, on purpose: a live call has to route your real network address to a media server that can't hide behind the usual protections, so it exposes who's on a call together and from where — the one thing the rest of the design works hardest to avoid. It may arrive later as an optional, clearly-labeled add-on for communities that accept that trade-off. For audio in the meantime, send an encrypted voice note (the paperclip / microphone in the compose box) — it rides the same end-to-end encryption as any other message.
