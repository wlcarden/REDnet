Know your rights & legal support

Before an action: read the guides. If someone is arrested: legal support and bail.

  • ACLU — Protesters' RightsNational
    Your First Amendment rights before, during, and after a demonstration — what police can and can't do.
    aclu.org/know-your-rights/protesters-rights
  • ACLU of DC — Demonstrations in D.C.DC-metro
    Know-your-rights for protests in Washington, DC, including permit rules and before/during/after guidance.
    acludc.org/know-your-rights
  • National Lawyers Guild — Mass Defense ProgramNational
    Trains and deploys legal observers, runs know-your-rights trainings and jail support, and finds representation for arrested protesters through local chapters.
    nlg.org/massdefenseprogram · massdef@nlg.org
  • NLG Washington, DC Chapter — Legal SupportDC-metro
    The DC chapter of the National Lawyers Guild — protest legal observers, jail support, and arrest support for DC-area actions.
    Request intake: dcmassdefense@gmail.com · nlgdc.org A hotline, 202-850-0010, is listed on the NLG national chapter directory. The DC chapter directs new requests to the email above, and protest hotlines are often stood up per event — confirm the current arrest-support number with NLG-DC before an action rather than assuming this one is staffed.
  • National Bail Fund Network — DirectoryNational
    Directory of 90+ community bail and bond funds across the US — use it to find a fund that can post bail in your area.
    communityjusticeexchange.org/en/nbfn-directory
  • Baltimore Action Legal Team — Community Bail FundMaryland
    A community bail fund serving Baltimore and Maryland, operating since 2015.
    baltimoreactionlegal.org/community-bail-fund
Digital security

Protect your phone, your accounts, and the people you organize with.

  • EFF — Surveillance Self-DefenseNational
    Practical, plain-language digital-security guides from the Electronic Frontier Foundation — device encryption, turning off biometric unlock before a protest, and encrypted messaging with Signal.
    ssd.eff.org · Protest guide: Attending a Protest · Activist or Protester playlist
  • Access Now — Digital Security HelplineNational / intl
    Free, confidential digital-security help for activists, journalists, and human-rights defenders — both preventive advice and rapid response if you think you've been targeted.
    accessnow.org/help · help@accessnow.org No phone line — reach them by email or the web form. They aim to respond within ~2 hours (initial reply, not a fix), and support is for members of civil society.
  • Freedom of the Press Foundation — Digital SecurityNational
    Journalist-centered guides — encrypting messages, locking down accounts and devices, resisting hacking and harassment — much of which applies directly to activists too.
    freedom.press/digisec
Mutual aid & documenting abuses

Find support near you, and know your rights to record what happens.

  • Mutual Aid HubNational
    A searchable map to find mutual-aid networks and community support projects near you — join efforts, offer resources, or request help.
    mutualaidhub.org
  • Serve Your City / Ward 6 Mutual AidDC-metro
    A Washington, DC nonprofit and hub for #DCMutualAid, coordinating neighbor-to-neighbor support across the city.
    serveyourcitydc.org
  • ACLU — Recording & Documenting PoliceNational
    Your right to film police and federal agents in public, and how to document what you see safely.
    aclu.org/know-your-rights/recording-and-documenting-police-and-federal-agents
  • U.S. Press Freedom TrackerNational
    A Freedom of the Press Foundation project documenting press-freedom violations in the US — arrests, assaults, and equipment seizures, including at protests.
    pressfreedomtracker.us
